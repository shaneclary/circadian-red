The Science

Melanopsin Sensitivity

Human circadian systems operate through melanopsin photopigment with peak sensitivity at 479nm. The 446-477nm range suppresses melatonin with devastating efficiency - requiring only 30 lux for measurable disruption in sensitive individuals.

Pollack's Structured Water

Research by Gerald Pollack shows 450-500nm blue light actively destroys cellular structured water (EZ water), while red and infrared wavelengths enhance it. The same wavelengths disrupting circadian rhythm also damage cellular hydration.

Red Light Safety

Wavelengths above 620nm show <10% melanopsin activation. Studies show 631nm red allows melatonin recovery after 2 hours, while blue maintains suppression. Target intensity: <3 lux at eye position.
